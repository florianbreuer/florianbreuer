Theorem 17.1.3. Properties of Scalar Multiplication and Matrix Addition.

Let \(A\text{,}\) \(B\) and \(C\) be matrices of the same size. Then

(A1)
\(A+B = B+A \hspace{32mm}\) (Commutative Law)
(A2)
\((A+B)+C = A+(B+C) \hspace{10mm}\)(Associative Law)
(A3)
\(A+0 = A \hspace{57mm}\) (Identity Law)
(A4)
\(A+(-A) = 0 \hspace{50mm}\) (Inverse Law)
(S1)
\(k(A+B) = kA+kB\)
(S2)
\((k_1 + k_2)A= k_1A + k_2A\)
(S3)
\(k_1(k_2A) = (k_1k_2)A\)
(S4)
\(1A = A\)
