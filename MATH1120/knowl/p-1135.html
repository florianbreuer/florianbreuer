For the square matrix \(A\text{,}\) the eigenvalues and associated eigenvectors are defined by the equation

\begin{equation} A\mathbf{x}=\lambda\mathbf{x}\tag{19.2.1} \end{equation}

Thus, we want to find values of \(\lambda\text{,}\) and associated vectors \(\mathbf{x}\text{,}\) that satisfy this equation. To do this, rearrange (19.2.1) to obtain

\begin{equation*} A\mathbf{x}-\lambda\mathbf{x}=\mathbf{0} \end{equation*}

which can be written as

\begin{equation*} A\mathbf{x}-\lambda I\mathbf{x}=\mathbf{0} \end{equation*}

or

\begin{equation} (A-\lambda I)\mathbf{x}=\mathbf{0}\tag{19.2.2} \end{equation}

where \(I\) is the identity matrix of order \(n\text{.}\) Since (19.2.2) is a homogeneous system of linear equations it will only have a non-zero solution when

\begin{equation} \det(A-\lambda I)=0\tag{19.2.3} \end{equation}
