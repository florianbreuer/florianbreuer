Item 3.

Since (17.4.1) has an infinite number of solutions let \(\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{u}\) and \(\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{v}\) be two distinct solutions to (17.4.1). Now let \(\mathbf{w} = \mathbf{u} − \mathbf{v}\) and \(t \in \mathbb{R}\text{.}\) Then
\begin{equation*} A(t\mathbf{w}) = tA(\mathbf{u} − \mathbf{v}) = t(A\mathbf{u} − A\mathbf{v}) = t(\mathbf{b} − \mathbf{b}) = \mathbf{0}\text{,} \end{equation*}
i.e. there are an infinite number of solutions to (17.4.2).
