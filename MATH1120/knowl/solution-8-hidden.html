Once again we will use the Maclaurin series for \(f(x)=\dfrac{1}{1-x}\) on \(\lvert x\rvert < 1. \) Now

\begin{align*} \frac{x^2}{2-5x} \amp =\dfrac{x^{2}}{2}(\dfrac{1}{1-(\frac{5x}{2})})\\ \amp = \dfrac{x^2}{2}(1+(\frac{5x}{2})+(\frac{5x}{2})^{2}+(\frac{5x}{2})^{3}+\cdots)\\ \amp = \dfrac{x^2}{2}\sum_{k=0}^{\infty}(\dfrac{5x}{2})^{k} \end{align*}

which will converge on the interval \(\lvert \frac{5x}{2}\rvert < 1, \) i.e., \(-\frac{2}{5}< x < \frac{2}{5}. \)
