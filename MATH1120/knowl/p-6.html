Notice that the linear approximation, \(L(x)\text{,}\) is the function obtained by truncating the infinite series representation for \(e^x\) (given above) after two terms. If we were to take the first three terms of the series we would have a quadratic approximation for \(e^x\text{,}\) i.e.

\begin{equation*} Q(x) = 1 + x + \frac{x^2}{2}. \end{equation*}
