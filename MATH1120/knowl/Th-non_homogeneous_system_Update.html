Theorem 18.2.10.

Consider the non-homogenous system of \(n \) linear equations in \(n \) variables
\begin{equation*} A \bm{x} = \bm{b},\; \bm{b} \neq 0. \end{equation*}
The following statements are equivalent:

  1. The system has a unique solution,

  2. \(A \bm{x} =0 \) has only the trivial solution \(\bm{x} =0, \)

  3. The columns of \(A \) are linearly independent,

  4. \(A \) is invertible.

  5. \(\det(A)\neq 0 \text{.}\)
