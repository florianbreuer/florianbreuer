Definition 15.2.1.

An \(m\times n\) matrix is a rectangular array of numbers with \(m\) rows and \(n\) columns, i.e.

\begin{equation*} \begin{pmatrix} a_{11} \amp a_{12} \amp \dots \amp a_{1n} \\ a_{21} \amp a_{22} \amp \dots \amp a_{2n} \\ \vdots \amp \vdots \amp \ddots \amp \vdots \\ a_{m1} \amp a_{m2} \amp \dots \amp a_{mn} \end{pmatrix} \end{equation*}

We say that the number \(a_{ij}\) is the \((i,j)\)th entry (i.e. the entry in row \(i\text{,}\) column \(j\)).
