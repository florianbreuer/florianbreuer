Theorem 18.1.9. Properties of the Matrix Invers.

Let \(A \) and \(B \) be square invertible matrices of order \(n \) and let \(k \) be a real number. Then

  1. \(\displaystyle (A^{-1})^{-1}=A \)

  2. \(\displaystyle (A^{T})^{-1}=(A^{-1})^{T} \)

  3. \(\displaystyle (kA)^{-1}=\frac{1}{k}A^{-1} \)

  4. \(\displaystyle (AB)^{-1}=B^{-1}A^{-1} \)

  5. \(\displaystyle (A^{r})^{-1}=(A^{-1})^{r}\;\;\;\; \text{where}\;\; r\in \mathbb{R} \)
