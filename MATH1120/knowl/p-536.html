Returning to equation (8.3.1), on differentiating both sides with respect to \(t\text{,}\) we obtain

\begin{alignat*}{1} \dfrac{dz}{dt} \amp= \dfrac{d}{dt}(0)\\ F_x + F_y \dfrac{dy}{dt} \amp = 0. \end{alignat*}

Of course \(x=t\) so \(\dfrac{dy}{dx} = \dfrac{dy}{dt}\) from which we obtain, provided \(F_y \neq 0\text{,}\)

\begin{equation*} \dfrac{dy}{dx} = -\dfrac{F_x}{F_y}. \end{equation*}
