Solution 18.1.4.1.

Begin by augmenting matrix \(A \) with the identity matrix \(I, \)
\begin{equation*} \begin{pmatrix} 1 \amp 1 \amp \bigm | \amp 1 \amp 0 \\ 1 \amp 2 \amp \bigm | \amp 0 \amp 1 \end{pmatrix} \end{equation*}
Now use the elementary row operations to reduce this to reduced row echelon form
\begin{align*} \begin{pmatrix} 1 \amp 1 \amp \bigm | \amp 1 \amp 0 \\ 1 \amp 2 \amp \bigm | \amp 0 \amp 1 \end{pmatrix} \amp \sim \begin{pmatrix} 1 \amp 1 \amp \bigm | \amp 1 \amp 0 \\ 0 \amp 1 \amp \bigm | \amp -1 \amp 1 \end{pmatrix} \;\; R'_{2}= R_{2}-R_{1} \\ \amp \sim \begin{pmatrix} 1 \amp 0 \amp \bigm | \amp 2 \amp -1 \\ 0 \amp 1 \amp \bigm | \amp -1 \amp 1 \end{pmatrix} \;\; R'_{1}= R_{1}-R_{2}. \end{align*}
We can read off the inverse as
\begin{equation*} A^{-1}=\begin{pmatrix} 2 \amp -1 \\ -1 \amp 1 \end{pmatrix}. \end{equation*}
