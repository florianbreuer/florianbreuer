Theorem 18.1.6.

Given the square matrix \(A \text{,}\) to find its inverse \(A^{-1} \text{:}\)

  1. Form the matrix \(\begin{pmatrix} A \bigm | I \\ \end{pmatrix} \) by augmenting \(A \) with the identity matrix \(I, \)

  2. Row reduce \(\begin{pmatrix} A \bigm | I \\ \end{pmatrix} \) to reduced row echelon form,

  3. If the reduced row echelon matrix is of the form \(\begin{pmatrix} I \bigm | A^{-1} \\ \end{pmatrix} \) read off \(A^{-1} \text{.}\) Otherwise the inverse does not exist.
