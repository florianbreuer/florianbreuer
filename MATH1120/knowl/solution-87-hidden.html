Let the curve be \(y(x)\text{.}\) Then we know

\begin{equation*} y'=\frac{2x}{y^2}, \hspace{5mm} y(0)=3 \end{equation*}

i.e. \(y\) is the solution to an initial-value problem involving a first order separable DE. From the DE, we obtain

\begin{equation*} \int y^2 \hspace{2mm}dy=\int 2x\hspace{2mm}dx \end{equation*}

and hence

\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{3}y^3=x^2+C \end{equation*}

Using the initial condition gives \(C=9\) and hence the required curve is

\begin{equation*} y(x)=(3x^2+27)^{1/3} \end{equation*}
