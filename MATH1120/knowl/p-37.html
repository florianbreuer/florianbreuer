It can be shown, via the ratio test, that the series converges for \(0 < x <2\text{.}\) Firstly,

\begin{equation*} L =\lim_{k\to\infty}\left|\dfrac{(x-1)^{k+1}}{k+2}\cdot\dfrac{k+1}{(x-1)^k}\right| = |x-1|. \end{equation*}

Thus \(L<1\) when \(|x-1|<1\text{,}\) i.e., \(0 < x <2\text{.}\)
