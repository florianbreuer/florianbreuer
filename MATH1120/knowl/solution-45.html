Solution 6.1.5.1.

For the given function

\begin{equation*} f_x = \cos(x+2y) \: \text{ and } \: f_y = 2\cos(x+2y)\text{.} \end{equation*}

Now, the unit vector in the direction of the angle \(\theta = \dfrac{3 \pi}{4}\) is

\begin{equation*} \hat{\bm{u}} = \left \langle - \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}}, \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \right \rangle\text{.} \end{equation*}

Thus the required directional derivative is

\begin{align*} D_\hat{\bm{u}} f(x,y) \amp = \cos(x+2y) \left( - \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \right) + 2\cos(x+2y) \left( \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \right)\\ \amp = \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \cos(x+2y) \end{align*}
