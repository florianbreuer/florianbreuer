For \(f(x)=\cos(x)\text{,}\)

\begin{alignat*}{1} f(x) \amp = \cos(x) \text{ and hence } f(0)=1\\ f'(x) \amp = -\sin(x) \text{ and hence } f'(0)=0\\ f''(x) \amp = -\cos(x) \text{ and hence } f''(0)=-1\\ f'''(x) \amp = \sin(x) \text{ and hence } f'''(0)=0\\ f^{(iv)}(x) \amp = \cos(x) \text{ and hence } f^{(iv)}(0)=1 \end{alignat*}

and so the \(0\)th degree Maclaurin polynomial is

\begin{equation*} M_0(x)=f(0)=1. \end{equation*}

Notice that the \(0\)th degree Maclaurin polynomial is the horizontal line passing through the point \((0,f(0))\text{.}\)
