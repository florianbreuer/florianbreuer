To locate the critical points we solve the equations \(z_x = z_y = 0\text{,}\) and check for any points where they do not exist. Since this function is given by a polynomial, the partial derivatives exist. We set them to zero below.

\begin{align} 6x^2 + y^2 + 10x \amp = 0\tag{7.1.1}\\ 2(x+1)y \amp = 0\tag{7.1.2} \end{align}

From (7.1.2)

\begin{equation*} x=-1 \: \text{ or } \: y=0\text{.} \end{equation*}

Putting \(y=0\) into (7.1.1) gives

\begin{equation*} x=0 \: \text{ or } \: x=-\dfrac{5}{3}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Putting \(x=-1\) into (7.1.1) gives

\begin{equation*} y=2 \: \text{ or } \: y=-2\text{.} \end{equation*}

Thus there are 4 critical points at

\begin{equation*} \left( 0, 0 \right), \left( -\dfrac{5}{3}, 0 \right), \left( -1, 2 \right) \: \text{ and } \left( -1, -2 \right)\text{.} \end{equation*}
