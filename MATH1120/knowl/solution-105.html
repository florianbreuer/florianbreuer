The general solution to this DE, found via an integrating factor, is

\begin{equation*} y(x)=Ae^x+xe^x \end{equation*}

Letting

\begin{equation*} y_c(x)=Ae^x \textrm{ and } y_p(x)=xe^x \end{equation*}

we see that, once again, \(y_c(x)\) is the general solution to the associated homogeneous DE but this time \(y_p(x)\) is not quite the same form as \(f(x)\text{.}\) The difference in this case from the previous two examples is that here \(f(x)\) is the same form as \(y_c(x)\text{,}\) the solution to the homogeneous DE and hence \(y_p(x)\) canâ€™t also be of this form.
