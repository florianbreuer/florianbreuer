As illustrated in ExampleÂ 19.2.8, for an upper triangular matrix the eigenvalues are the entries on the main diagonal. Unfortunately, we cannot use row reduction to find eigenvalues because equivalent matrices do not necessarily have the same eigenvalues. Interestingly though, it can be shown that sum of the eigenvalues of any square matrix is equal to the sum of the entries on the main diagonal (i.e. to the trace of the matrix).
