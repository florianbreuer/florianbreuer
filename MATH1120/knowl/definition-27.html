Definition 14.1.2. Hooke's Law.

If a spring is stretched (compressed) \(U\) units beyond its natural position then it pulls (pushes) with a force, \(F_r\), of magnitude

\begin{equation*} \vert F_r\vert =kU, \hspace{5mm} k>0 \end{equation*}
where the value of \(k\) depends on the properties of the spring itself.
