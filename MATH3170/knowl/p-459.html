Let \(p\) be an odd prime and let \(a\) and \(b\) be coprime to \(p.\) Then following properties hold:

  1. If \(a\equiv b\pmod{p},\) then \((a/p) = (b/p).\)

  2. \(\displaystyle (a^2/p) = 1.\)

  3. \(\displaystyle (a/p)\equiv a^{(p-1)/2}\pmod{p}.\)

  4. \(\displaystyle (ab/p)=(a/p)(b/p).\)

  5. \(\displaystyle (1/p)\equiv 1 \hspace{2mm} \mathrm{and} \hspace{2mm} (-1/p)=(-1)^{(p-1)/2}.\)
