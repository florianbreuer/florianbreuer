Theorem 9.3.3.

If \(p\) is an odd prime, then

\begin{equation*} (2/p)= \begin{cases} 1 \amp \hspace{5mm} if \hspace{2mm} p\equiv 1\pmod{8} \hspace{2mm} or \hspace{2mm} p\equiv 7\pmod{8}\\ -1 \amp \hspace{5mm} if \hspace{2mm} p\equiv 3\pmod{8} \hspace{2mm} or \hspace{2mm} p\equiv 7\pmod{8}. \end{cases} \end{equation*}

Proof.
