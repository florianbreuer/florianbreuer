Let \(\varepsilon_1, \varepsilon_2,\cdots, \varepsilon_g\) be arbitrary numbers each equal to \(+1\) or \(-1,\) where \(g\) is a fixed (but arbitrary) number. Then there exists an \(n\) such that

\begin{equation*} \lambda(n+m)=\varepsilon_m \hspace{5mm} (1\leq m\leq g). \end{equation*}
